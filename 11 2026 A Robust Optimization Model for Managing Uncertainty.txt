ABSTRACT The integration of distributed energy resources (DER), such as solar photovoltaic (PV)
panels and electric vehicle (EV) parking facilities, has significantly improved power grid flexibility and
efficiency. These resources not only enhance grid management but also enable consumers to reduce costs
and generate additional income by trading surplus energy through peer-to-peer (P2P) energy trading.
However, the effective utilization of these resources requires adopted and developed optimization strategies
to manage operational uncertainties, particularly the variability of renewable energy production, which
directly influences electricity market prices. This paper presents an optimization approach for managing
risks posed by various uncertain parameters such as the renewable energy-based consumer systems, the
availability of EVs in the parking lots, and market prices. In this paper, the uncertainties posed from the
renewable resources and EV availability in the parking lots addressed through stochastic programming while
the electricity market prices are managed by robust optimization. Electricity prices, as uncertain parameters,
can significantly impact decision variables. Since consumers are price-takers rather than price-makers, they
have no direct control over market prices, making it crucial for risk-averse decision-makers to address price
volatility effectively. By analyzing uncertainties and modeling various scenarios, the proposed approach
ensures optimal decision-making under different risk conditions. To evaluate its effectiveness, the model is
validated on a case study and also on through a factorial sensitivity analysis with 27 scenarios in a microgrid
with multiple households. The optimal configuration (10 homes, 150% PV, 200% ESS) achieves a 38.3%
cost reduction compared to the baseline This study provides a comprehensive analysis of the simulation
outcomes and real-world implementation, offering valuable insights into improving P2P energy trading in
the face of price fluctuations.

I. INTRODUCTION

In recent years, concerns over air pollution, primarily driven
by fossil fuel-based power generation and the increasing accumulation of greenhouse gases, have encouraged
many consumers to seek energy self-sufficiency. To achieve
this goal, they are increasingly turning to DERs as a
means of meeting their electricity demands while promoting a cleaner environment [1]. However, integrating these
resources presents significant challenges, particularly in managing excess energy production. For example, renewable
energy sources such as rooftop PV panels and wind turbines may generate surplus electricity during periods of low
demand. Due to factors such as the absence of adequate
battery storage or limited storage capacity, this excess energy
often remains underutilized or is wasted. Consequently, these
inefficiencies lead to increased energy losses and reduced
overall system performance [2].
One of the most significant advancements in the energy
sector is the adoption of peer-to-peer (P2P) energy trading,
which offers an effective solution to the challenges associated with surplus energy management. This approach enables
individuals to sell excess electricity to households with
higher energy demands, thereby improving energy efficiency.
By facilitating localized energy exchanges, P2P trading also
contributes to environmental sustainability by decreasing
reliance on fossil fuel-based power generation and lowering
greenhouse gas emissions [3]. Additionally, it promotes a
more decentralized and resilient energy system while providing financial benefits to prosumers who generate surplus
electricity. It is worthwhile to mention that prosumer is a
household that both consumes electricity and is equipped
with local generation and/or storage (e.g., PV and EV), and

can therefore act as both a buyer and a seller in the P2P trading
scheme.
On the other hand, another transformative development in
the energy sector is the rapid expansion of electric vehicle
(EV) technology. In 2023 alone, global sales of EVs surpassed 13.7 million units, reflecting the increasing adoption
of sustainable transportation solutions [4]. The declining cost
of EVs, driven by technological advancements and market
competition, has been a key factor in accelerating this transition. As EVs become more affordable and efficient, they
present new opportunities for integrating renewable energy
sources into transportation, further enhancing the sustainability of modern energy systems.
Despite the potential benefits that the power grid can derive
from the large-scale deployment of solar energy systems
and EVs, the incentives provided by electricity markets and
utility companies to promote the growth of these technologies
remain insufficiently encouraging. Thus, P2P energy trading
has emerged as a next-generation energy management technique for power grids. This approach empowers consumers
to actively participate in the energy market in two key paths:
by selling their surplus energy or by reducing their energy
demand. The significance of the P2P energy trading model
has been clearly emphasized in a recent study referenced
in [5]. This study develops an optimization model based on
P2P energy trading to evaluate the role of this approach in
reducing carbon emissions in Finland by 2050. The central hypothesis assumes that each of the 278 municipalities
considered will establish an energy community comprising
four competitive sectors, each acting as distinct stakeholders. These four sectors play a crucial role in determining
energy production and consumption levels, energy transfer,
and carbon emission reductions. Through P2P energy trading, these sectors are enabled to exchange energy and share
resources, fostering more effective energy management and
sustainability.
In this section, we aim to provide a comprehensive review
of recent studies and advancements in the field of P2P energy
trading. One notable study introduces a novel framework for
P2P energy trading markets, built on a multi-sided economic
distribution model. This innovative framework, presented
in [6], leverages a relaxed consensus approach combined with
innovative techniques to address the studied challenges and
enhance market performance. In practice, this new market
model innovatively transforms energy trading by enabling
direct interactions between consumers and producers based
on their preferences. It facilitates more efficient utilization of
energy resources while simultaneously enhancing social welfare. This research not only contributes to improving energy
market performance but also reduces reliance on traditional
energy sources and helps lower greenhouse gas emissions.
These findings highlight that recent advancements in P2P
energy trading are driving significant changes in the energy
industry. In [7], a game theory-based approach is explored
and applied within the context of P2P energy trading in a
smart grid. This method is designed to achieve objectives

such as reducing greenhouse gas emissions and minimizing
energy-related costs. By incentivizing and encouraging users
to actively participate in P2P energy trading, this approach
aims to enhance the performance of smart grids and improve
energy efficiency. The study demonstrates that employing
motivational game theory in energy trading can facilitate the
design of dynamic and flexible smart grids, advancing sustainable energy and environmental goals. Such approaches,
by integrating economic and environmental factors, increase
user engagement and contribute to improving the energy
balance of the country.
In addition, a centralized system is highlighted for its
capability to assist in reducing electricity demand during
peak hours in [8]. Specifically, this study implements a P2P
energy trading scheme using a cooperative Stackelberg game
formulation. This approach enables the power system to
achieve greater efficiency by managing electricity demand
during peak times, thereby alleviating stress on the grid
and improving overall system performance. To address the
challenge of reducing power demand during critical periods,
studies in [9] propose a subscription-based tariff structure.
This tariff model aims to optimize energy consumption by
improving efficiency and better managing demand, thereby
reducing the power load on the energy distribution system
during critical times. In another study, a two-stage strategy
for energy sharing among buildings has been developed to
resolve the energy-sharing issue. This strategy operates in
two phases and focuses on enhancing economic benefits for
energy-consuming buildings. The proposed energy-sharing
approach not only ensures more efficient utilization of shared
energy resources but also provides economic advantages to
building owners and residents [10]. Hahnel et al. in [11]
focused on customer preferences in the context of P2P energy
trading. This research aimed to provide a clearer understanding of homeowner behavior and the factors influencing their
decisions within P2P energy trading systems. The primary
objective of the study was to enhance transaction volumes in
decentralized energy systems. Through the analysis of data
collected from homeowners in Germany, the results revealed
that key factors influencing their trading behavior included
the state of charge in private storage units and the electricity
prices within the community. These factors play a significant
role in shaping customer decisions and increasing their trading activities in P2P energy markets.
Additionally, in [12], the authors propose a novel approach
to enhance end-user consumption by aggregating and developing rules aimed at reducing system costs. This innovative
method plays a crucial role in optimizing and minimizing
system-related expenses, contributing significantly to achieving improved outcomes for end-users. In [13], an entirely
decentralized and innovative market mechanism is introduced
that respects the preferences and needs of buyers. This mechanism considers critical factors such as buyersŌĆÖ preferences
for participating in renewable energy trading, the reputation
of their trading partners, and their position within the network. By doing so, consumers are allowed to participate

model also considers uncertainties and the utilization of electric vehicles in its framework.
Furthermore, the authors in [17] presented a P2P energy
trading mechanism that incorporates two types of final pricing: a preferred final price and an uncertain local final price.
While the study accounts for uncertainties in renewable
energy sources during the initial stage, it does not consider
uncertainties in load consumption. Through this mechanism,
participants in P2P energy trading can choose and accept
suitable prices for buying and selling energy, enabling them
to achieve greater efficiency in managing renewable energy.
This approach improves the performance of P2P energy
trading markets by addressing price uncertainties and accommodating participantsŌĆÖ preferences. In [18], a centralized
optimization algorithm is employed in the context of P2P
energy trading, focusing on pricing factors and the charging
and discharging behaviors of electric vehicles to encourage
participation in the market. While the primary focus is on
electric vehicles and their management, renewable energy
sources are not comprehensively integrated into the model,
and the uncertainties associated with these sources are also
not accounted for in this framework. This algorithm can promote improved performance in P2P energy trading markets;
however, further development and refinement are needed
to incorporate additional factors, such as renewable energy
sources and market uncertainties.
According to [19], a local network system has been
designed and modeled to include 50 households, each
equipped with electric vehicles and solar panels for electricity
generation. These households are interconnected through a
P2P energy trading platform. The authors of this study utilize the concept of bidirectional chargers to facilitate energy

transfer. These chargers enable the flow of energy between
electric vehicles and homes, allowing the energy stored in
vehicle batteries to be discharged back into homes or the
grid. Additionally, energy storage capabilities are incorporated into the model. However, the primary limitation of
this model is its inability to account for the uncertainties
associated with user energy consumption. In other words, the
model cannot manage variations arising from the instability
of user energy demand. This uncertainty could significantly
impact decision-making strategies within the model, highlighting the need for modifications to enhance its accuracy
and efficiency. In article [20], a P2P energy trading platform is introduced as a generalized game. This platform is
designed for buyers, with a network operator responsible for
enforcing system operational constraints. Additionally, the
study employs a distributed market clearing mechanism to
manage energy transactions and provide buyers with access
to energy. However, critical factors such as electric vehicles,
photovoltaic systems, and uncertainties were not considered
in the development and design of this model. This omission
could significantly affect the modelŌĆÖs accuracy and performance. Therefore, to enhance the precision and efficiency of
this model, it is essential to incorporate these factors as fundamental elements in future analyses and modeling efforts.
In addition, the authors in [21] examined a P2P energy
trading scheme for multiple microgrids operating under
uncertainty, accompanied by the proposal of a bi-level optimization framework. This framework facilitates interactions
between P2P transactions under uncertain conditions and
power flows managed by distribution system operators.
To enhance operational security and reduce power losses
within local energy distribution networks, an optimal power
flow model is developed at the upper level, designed to
align power flows optimally and minimize energy losses.
At the lower level, a stochastic programming-based P2P
trading model is implemented to address trading within multiple microgrids. To solve the proposed framework, a nested
two-level distributed algorithm, integrated with a cascading algorithm, is utilized. This approach ensures distributed
solutions while maintaining consistency in decision-making
across both levels and between microgrids. However, the
framework does not account for renewable energy sources,
which could greatly improve the flexibility and adaptability
of P2P trading. This limitation highlights the need for integrating renewable energy sources to enhance the efficiency
and effectiveness of P2P energy trading models.
The authors in [22] proposed an energy management system for a hypothetical community equipped with all the
necessary components for P2P energy trading. These components include consumers with renewable energy generation
units, a centralized energy storage facility, and a regional
power grid system. The study introduces two key optimization frameworks for managing the distribution of surplus
energy produced locally. The first framework is based on a
decision-making game theory model, enabling optimal utilization by consumers at a lower level and shared storage

One of the primary challenges in this domain is motivating and encouraging buyers and consumers to actively

associated with fluctuating demand in P2P energy transactions. Therefore, it is essential to carefully address these
challenges in the modeling of P2P energy trading systems.
This focus is necessary not only for the optimal management
of risks related to demand uncertainties but also to reduce
the overall operational costs associated with P2P trading
platforms. Consequently, these considerations highlight the
importance of accurately addressing these challenges in the
design and modeling of P2P energy trading. By doing so,
greater efficiency and flexibility can be achieved in this
emerging field. In P2P energy trading platforms, uncertainty
is one of the most critical and challenging issues. This uncertainty becomes particularly significant when it pertains to
fluctuations in electricity market prices for end users, as it
can greatly impact the efficiency and performance of such
platforms. For instance, sudden and unexpected changes in
energy demand can lead to instability and operational issues
within these systems. Unfortunately, studies such as [16],
[20], and [23] have overlooked this type of uncertainty and
its implications. Future research must give greater attention to
this matter, aiming to improve models and methods for managing such uncertainties. Addressing this issue effectively
could enhance the efficiency and stability of P2P energy trading platforms while providing better solutions for handling
sudden changes in energy demand. Additionally, incorporating electric vehicles into energy systems could offer greater
flexibility for end users. This flexibility would allow them to
participate more actively in P2P trading activities that might
have been previously neglected or deemed impractical. This
highlights the importance of managing and utilizing electric energy resources in P2P trading systems, as emphasized
in [20] and [24]. Such an approach can contribute to the
development of more adaptable and resilient energy trading
frameworks. However, it is important to note that while there

and wind. The framework provides strategies that balance
risk-averse decision-making, extending prior studies that usually consider only one type of uncertainty.

II. THE MODEL AND MATHEMATICAL PROBLEM
FORMULATION

In this section, we present the model and the mathematical
formulation of the local aggregatorŌĆÖs decision-making process in the presence of electricity market price uncertainties.
As illustrated in Fig. 1, inside the microgrid, there are several
households which are considered as prosumers that are connected to the upstream network and a central energy storage
unit. Moreover, each household has PV, small-scale wind turbine and EV. The operator plays a critical role in coordinating
energy transactions and optimizing resource allocation within
a localized energy network.
Considering the stochastic nature of market prices and
the intermittency of renewable energy generation, and availability of EVs in the parking lot, we adopt a hybrid
stochastic-robust uncertainty modeling approach. In the proposed formulation, robust optimization is applied to the
uncertainty of electricity market prices, while the variability of renewable generation from PV and wind, as well
as the available capacity of the EV parking lot, is represented through a set of scenarios, each with an associated
probability. This hybrid treatment enhances the operatorŌĆÖs
decision-making process and ensures operational feasibility
and economic efficiency under uncertainty. By explicitly
modeling these uncertainties, the framework allows the
decision-maker to adapt to fluctuating market conditions,
mitigate financial risks, and maintain a reliable energy supply
for participants in the P2P trading environment.

C. ROBUST OPTIMIZATION MODEL

As discussed earlier, uncertainty is a fundamental challenge
in power system operation, particularly in DERs where
renewable generation and market dynamics introduce significant variability. Accurate planning is challenging due to
fluctuating electricity prices and the intermittent nature of
VOLUME 14, 2026

S. Seyyedeh-Barhagh, M. Abapour: Robust Optimization Model for Managing Uncertainty in LEM Prices

renewable sources such as solar irradiance and wind speed.
The risks associated from the renewables and the availability
of EVs in the parking lots are addressed though the stochastic
programming, where multiple scenarios with specific probabilities for the uncertain parameters are generated.
For modeling another source of uncertainty, electricity
market price, a robust optimization model is employed [37].
This method evaluates various scenarios, to determine an
optimal strategy for the decision-makers in different levels of conservatism. This study utilizes a combination of
stochastic programming and robust optimization (hybrid
robust-stochastic) to handle uncertainties in renewable energy
generation, the availabilities of EVs in the parking lots
and electricity market prices. While stochastic programming
creates multiple possible scenarios based on probability distributions, robust optimization ensures that decisions remain
feasible even in the most unfavorable conditions. Thus, this
approach enhances the stability and economic efficiency of
the system.

Figure 10 depicts the resulting cost surface as a function
of PV penetration and ESS capacity for the three community sizes. Two main trends can be observed. First, for a
fixed number of homes, increasing PV penetration and ESS
capacity systematically reduces the annual cost per home,
with the largest savings obtained when both PV and ESS
are at 150 % and 200 % of their baseline values. Second,
for a fixed PV/ESS configuration, increasing the number of
participating homes from 4 to 8 and 10 reduces the cost per
home, revealing clear economies of scale in cooperative P2P
operation. The three colored planes in Fig. 10 are almost
parallel, indicating that the marginal benefit of PV and ESS
remains similar as the community grows, while the overall
cost level shifts downward when more prosumers participate
in the P2P trading scheme.
IV. CONCLUSION

This paper proposed a stochastic-robust optimization strategy to support decision-making in renewable energy-based
VOLUME 14, 2026

consumer (prosumers), with a particular focus on robust
optimization on the electricity price uncertainty. Given that
end-users typically act as price-takers and cannot influence
market prices directly, managing this uncertainty becomes
critical. The model developed in this work allows risk-averse
consumers to handle market price fluctuations more effectively, helping to protect their operational decisions against
volatile market conditions.
The primary objective was to minimize the total costs
while considering the uncertainty posed by the electricity
market prices. To manage market price uncertainty, a robust
optimization approach was employed, incorporating various levels of uncertainty. The results demonstrate that as
the uncertainty budget increases, the operational cost of the
proposed system also rises. However, the robustness of the
system against upstream grid price uncertainties improves
with the implementation of the robust strategy. Thus, the
microgrid decision-maker must balance the trade-off between
the robustness of the optimization plan and its associated
costs.
In future work, the framework can be extended from a centrally coordinated microgrid to decentralized or distributed
P2P energy management schemes and to settings where a
local aggregator coordinates several neighboring microgrids
and participates in external markets (e.g. wholesale energy or
flexibility markets). We also plan to investigate a combined
IGDT-robust-stochastic risk management scheme to better
capture the impact of increasing renewable penetration.
