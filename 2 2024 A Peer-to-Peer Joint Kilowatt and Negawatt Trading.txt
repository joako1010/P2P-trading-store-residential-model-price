A Peer-to-Peer Joint Kilowatt and Negawatt Trading
Framework Incorporating Battery
Cycling Degradation
Chenxi Zhang , Graduate Student Member, IEEE, Jing Qiu , Senior Member, IEEE,
and Yi Yang , Graduate Student Member, IEEE

Abstract—This paper presents a novel unified peer-to-peer (P2P)
framework for both kilowatt (kW) and negawatt (nW) trading. The
aim is to coordinate these two transaction types and maximize customer benefits, by reducing customers’ electricity bills and helping
customers trade energy with their neighbors while also adhering
to the physical limits of the electricity networks. An auction-based
joint kW and nW P2P trading approach is proposed to enable prosumers to conduct the optimal strategy in switching their market
roles between kW and nW trading and scheduling their demand.
As such, in the virtual layer, the household energy management
system (HEMS) models are formulated to determine the optimal
market bidding strategy during each trading period. For the agent
equipped with a battery energy storage system (BESS), the battery
cycling degradation issue is also considered through the embedded
battery cycle life model. A double-sided auction method with an
average pricing market (APM) mechanism is performed in P2P kW
and nW trading for market clearing. In the physical layer, voltage
issues and line congestion management are considered through the
distribution network models. The proposed formulation is tested
on the modified IEEE 33-bus distribution system and simulation
results demonstrate that the proposed framework outperforms the
single trading mode.
Index Terms—Peer-to-peer kilowatt and negawatt trading,
electricity cost reduction, battery cycle life, double-sided auction.

I. INTRODUCTION
WING to the rising penetration of distributed energy
resources (DERs), such as solar photovoltaic (PV), battery energy storage systems (BESSs), etc., along with recent
advances in communication and control infrastructure at the
residential level, traditional passive consumers are motivated
to turn into prosumers (proactive consumers) by controlling
their generation and demand [1], [2]. To facilitate local energy

the financial benefit of emerging prosumers, peer-to-peer (P2P)
trading has attracted widespread attention [3], [4].
In terms of operation mode and dispatch flexibility, P2P
trading can be classified into two categories, namely P2P kilowatt (kW) trading and P2P negawatt (nW) trading [5]. As a
potential alternative to the feed-in-tariff (FiT) scheme, P2P kW
trading is operated by surplus energy management, in which
kW buyers purchase shortage of demand from kW sellers with
redundant energy. The trading quantity and price are determined
according to the market settlement on a virtual platform such as
blockchain [6]. Thereafter, sellers should inject the agreed kW
quantities into the low-voltage (LV) distribution network, and
the corresponding kW amounts are consumed by buyers from
the connected network [7].
As a technique of energy management, the concept of nW was
first introduced in 1985 and defined as an electricity customer’s
right to buy energy [8]. During a shortage of supply, the energy
saved by reducing electricity consumption is denoted by nW
in this paper. An electricity consumer possesses the flexibility
to either reschedule energy-related activities to another time or
opt not to consume energy for certain activities. Subsequently,
they could sell their right to purchase the saved energy to others
within the network. P2P nW trading represents a trading mechanism wherein prosumers autonomously manage their energy
consumption behaviors to enhance the energy balance within
distribution networks. Demand response (DR) management is
typically organized by the electricity grid during a power supply
shortage. As a potential alternative to the DR strategy, P2P nW
trading is operated by energy demand rescheduling. In contrast
to the DR scheme, P2P nW trading empowers prosumers to
engage in direct trading with one another through P2P platforms.
Consequently, both sellers and buyers participating in P2P nW
transactions are anticipated to realize greater financial benefits
compared to the DR scheme. Based on the finalized quantities
and prices on the virtual platform, nW sellers need to decrease
their scheduled demand, and the excess demand is consumed by
nW buyers from the connected network correspondingly [5].
In the existing literature, P2P kW trading and nW trading
are usually studied and analyzed separately. Hereinto, the more
popular transaction form is P2P kW trading, in which various
technical approaches have been proposed in three main P2P

methods and auction-based methods are commonly used to
design a P2P kW system [13]. For example, the non-cooperative
game model in [14], [15] and the cooperative game model in [9]
have been exploited to simulate the behavior and interaction
of participants in the P2P market. In [16], a continuous double
auction (CDA) market framework is proposed, in which prosumers submit bids based on optimized trading strategies. A
double-auction mechanism is employed in [17] to promote the
collaboration between the proposed P2P trading platform and
DR schemes.
Different from the conventional DR programs, the rules and
decisions of which are mostly organized by the grid, nW trading
enables participants to trade with others in a prosumer-centric
way [18]. Owing to advantages in adjusting energy imbalance
and earning higher monetary gains for prosumers compared with
DR [19], a growing body of literature has investigated the design
of the P2P nW trading framework recently. For example, a novel
P2P nW trading transactive energy (TE) system is proposed in
[20] to address the problem of the increasing need to reduce energy consumption. In [21], a voltage-constraints P2P nW trading
framework applied in the LV distribution system is presented and
the superior financial performance of the proposed framework is
also proved compared with demand-side management (DSM). A
coalition game model utilized in cooperative P2P nW trading in
a grid-connected network is proposed in [22], in which the power
loss and voltage issues are considered. The effect of diminishing
electricity costs for prosumers has also been demonstrated to be
better compared with the DR scheme.
In the above literature, it can be found that either kW trading
mode or nW trading mode is feasible in the residential P2P
market to decrease energy costs and adjust the energy imbalance, hence, these two transaction types are able to exist in a
community market at the same time. Besides, participants in
the market without any DER who can merely act as buyers in
kW transactions might also become sellers in nW trading due
to the demand administration. However, there may be a lack of
a unified framework for both P2P kW and nW trading in the
distribution network to utilize the flexibility in both supply-side
and demand-side of residential prosumers and gather the benefits
of two trading types. Without the unified model, prosumers who
wish to engage in both P2P kW and nW trading simultaneously
may encounter significant challenges. These challenges arise
from separated optimization models that may yield entirely
different DERs scheduling plans and trading strategies, causing
residents’ confusion, and impeding their optimal role selection.
Furthermore, determining the appropriate bidding quantities in
both markets can be complex. This, in turn, diminishes arbitrage
opportunities and leads to profit losses of market participants as
well. Therefore, the need for a unified framework and model is
imperative to address these challenges.
To fill these existing research gaps, this paper proposes a
P2P joint kW and nW trading approach to coordinate two
transaction modes. The proposed models in this paper are not
a mere amalgamation of two existing standalone models, but
rather optimize and update market roles switching schemes,

and bidding strategies of prosumers through the current demand
status assessment and market feedback signals to achieve net
profit maximization. The battery cycling degradation issue and
the impact on the physical network, for example, line congestion
and overvoltage situation are considered in the proposed unified
models as well. Compared to the existing frameworks, the major
contributions of this paper are summarized as follows:
1) We propose a unified P2P kW and nW trading framework
that empowers all prosumers in the distribution system to
participate in both kW and nW transactions during each
trading interval. Each participant can switch their market
roles between kW and nW trading based on the bidding
strategy optimization results and the P2P market clearing
information.
2) We propose P2P joint kW and nW trading HEMS models
for prosumers to help each agent conduct the optimal
strategy in the following aspects: determining market roles
i.e., acting as buyer or seller between kW and nW trading, submitting market bids(offers), and adjusting their
demand. The battery cycle life model is embedded in
the HEMS models of prosumers equipped with BESS to
obtain the trade-off solution between price arbitrage by
charge-discharge cycles and battery life degradation.
3) A double-sided auction method with an APM mechanism
is proposed to clear the P2P kW and nW markets. The
effect of the trading results in the virtual layer on the
physical network is considered through the distribution
network models, for example, the voltage issue and power
line congestion. From the case study results, the superior performance of the proposed framework regarding
enlarging electricity consumption savings and easing the
pressure of the grid at peak times compared with the single
trading mode can be proved.
II. PROPOSED UNIFIED P2P KW AND NW TRADING
FRAMEWORK
A unified P2P kW and nW trading framework in a community
market is proposed in this paper to coordinate and improve the
synergies of two P2P transaction types, which can be applied
to a group of neighboring agents in a residential distribution
network. At each transaction interval, there are coexisting both
kW and nW trading modes in the P2P community market, the
framework of which consists of the virtual layer and the physical
layer, as shown in Fig. 1.
The virtual layer mainly incorporates the HEMS and market
operation process. It is assumed that all market participants
possess a smart meter and a HEMS. The data containing generation, demand, traded quantity and price, etc. of each agent
are recorded by the smart meter, which is linked with the
HEMS [22]. The data will be delivered from the smart meter
to the HEMS, which automatically handles the bid strategy
optimization, market roles switch, battery cycling scheduling,
and demand adjustment through an optimization algorithm. The
optimization process depends on the recorded data from the
smart meter, the informed market feedback signal, and the userpredefined objective. The market feedback signal includes the

threshold electricity procurement quantity in the retail market

by retailers or utilities on the amount of electricity users can
purchase in residential installations is very common. Let us
assume that purchasing thresholds have been imposed in this
paper. When a prosumer buys energy from the retail market, if
the purchasing amount at time t is not greater than the threshold
Dn,t , prosumer n can buy electricity in the retail market at

Now, in the proposed unified P2P framework, kW, nW and
retail markets coexist in the community at the same time. The
concept of P2P kW trading empowers each prosumer n with energy deficiency to purchase electricity from other DER-equipped
prosumers with surplus power. For the P2P nW trading, each
prosumer n with power scarcity (more than the threshold) is
entitled to buy the right of electricity procurement in the retail
market (at ToU price) from other prosumers who are willing to
reduce their demand. In this paper, we assume that all prosumers
are rational, driven by self-interest, willing to comply with the
trading strategies derived from the HEMS model, and nW sellers
are willing to sacrifice comfort to reduce demand for cost savings. Those who own excess generation or self-sufficiency may
lack the motivation and willingness to sacrifice their comforts
to act as an nW seller, hence in this paper, the nW trading is
mainly considered between prosumers with power deficiency at
each trading interval.

nW at each transaction slot depending on the power demand
status and the market feedback signal. The unbalanced power
needs to be traded in the retail market. The power selling price

Step 1: Each prosumer receives the ToU prices, higher prices,
and FiT, and their HEMS determines their net demand, BESS
scheduling plan, bids (offers) strategy, and market roles by
solving problems (8) and (10)–(35).
Step 2: The bids/offers and market roles of each prosumer are
collected, and the community manager clears the P2P kW
and nW market at the same time based on the double-sided
auction rules with the APM mechanism by solving problems
(36)–(43).
Step 3: The P2P kW and nW market clearing information of the
first-round bidding including the allocated kW/nW quantities
and MCP are released to each prosumer.
Step 4: The re-bidding process begins, based on the informed
market feedback signal of the first-round bidding, the reoptimization of each prosumer’s HEMS is conducted by
re-solving problems (8) and (10)–(35).
Step 5: The updated bids/offers and market roles of each prosumer are collected, and the community manager conducts
the auction and informs the market clearing results again by
re-solving problems (36)–(43).
Step 6: The re-bidding process continues until the deadline of
the trading slot, the final market clearing information, and
matching results are broadcast to all prosumers.

VI. CASE STUDY
In this paper, an iterative mixed-integer linear programming
is used to solve the proposed problem. The simulations were
conducted on a 64-bit laptop with a 2.80 GHz Intel Core
i7-1165G7 CPU and 16GB RAM. There are 11184 variables,
14160 inequality constraints, and 2376 equality constraints in
total. The optimization models are programmed and solved by
SCIP of OPTI toolbox in MATLAB. The benefit of utilizing
this solver lies in its remarkable capability to rapidly identify
feasible solutions.
A. Simulation Setup
1) Test System: The proposed P2P joint kW and nW trading
method is applied to a radial 33-node community network
modified based on the IEEE 33-bus distribution test feeder.
The network topology and layouts of agents and DERs are
illustrated in Fig. 4. There are 33 prosumers accommodated

in the community, 16 of them are outfitted with PV panels,
4 of them are equipped with PV panels and BESS, and the
remaining 13 prosumers are without any DER. Each household
is connected to one node.
2) Parameters Settings: The simulations are performed with
33 agents in total, T = 24 hours, each trading slot t = 1
hour. The historical load data is obtained from the Australia
Energy Market Operator (AEMO) and the historical PV generation profiles are procured from pvoutput.org. To handle the
uncertainty issue, a stochastic scenarios uncertainty prediction
method is utilized which has been described in detail in our past
work [31], in which generative adversarial networks (GANs)
are applied to generate future scenarios according to historical
data. Based on the generated scenario, the total energy demand
of the test community is 330kWh for one day with a peak
load of 25kW. Assume that the flexible power demand of each
prosumer accounts for 20% of the total demand, which can be
used for nW trading. The capital cost of BESS is 350$/kWh
and the rated energy capacity of the battery is 8.5 kWh. The
charging and discharging efficiency of the battery is 95% and
98%. The upper and lower bound of the SoC is 90% and 20%.
The maximum charging or discharging power is 1.5kW. The
total cycling numbers of the battery are 5000 times [31]. The
Peukert lifetime constant kp is set as 1. Based on the residential
ToU price in New South Wales (NSW), Australia, the peak
price is 48 cents/kWh, the shoulder price is 19.5 cents/kWh,
and the off-peak price is 12 cents/kWh. It is assumed that for
each prosumer, the purchase threshold from the retail market is
0.4 kW per hour, which is set by the community manager in
order to manage demand and avoid congestion. If the electricity
consumption of the prosumer is beyond the defined threshold,
1.6 times prices need to be paid compared with the ToU price
[22]. The FiT is selected as 4 cents/kWh depending on the
FiT schemes provided by retailers in NSW [32]. These power
transaction prices are reflected in Fig. 5. The secure voltage
range of all nodes is set to be [0.95 p.u., 1.05 p.u] [33].
3) Case Settings: In this paper, to verify the feasibility and
the effectiveness of the proposed P2P joint kW and nW trading
approach, 4 cases are established and specified as follows.
Base Case: The proposed unified P2P kW and nW trading
framework is utilized.
Case 1: Only P2P kW trading is considered in the community.
Case 2: Only P2P nW trading is considered in the community.
Case 3:The proposed unified P2P kW and nW trading framework is applied, however, the battery degradation model is
not embedded and used.
The establishment of cases 1 and 2 is aimed at comparing the
performance between the proposed trading framework and the
single trading mode. To be noted, the battery life cycle models
are all considered in the base case and cases 1 and 2. Case 3 is
to investigate the impact of ignoring battery life degradation.

VII. CONCLUSION
This paper proposes a novel unified P2P kW and nW trading framework to coordinate these two transaction types, in
which the P2P joint kW and nW trading HEMS models, the
double-sided auction models with APM mechanisms, and the
distribution network Distflow models are utilized. The battery
cycle life model is embedded in the proposed HEMS models
as well. The novel unified framework can effectively integrate
the generation-side and demand-side of both prosumers and
consumers, considering market price signals, the generation
condition of DERs, and the demand status. This coordination
allows all market participants to adapt their trading roles in each
market, freely engage in the retail market, P2P kW, and nW
markets, and further unleash residential flexibility.
The presented method is tested through the case study, and
its superiority is verified in terms of the following aspects: (1)
The market roles switch and demand schedule of prosumers can
be optimized; (2) The transaction results settled in the virtual
layer can comply with the restrictions in the physical layer; (3)
The compromised solution can be found to balance the battery
degradation and price arbitrage by charge-discharge cycles for
the prosumers outfitted with BESS; (4) Compared with the single
trading mode, the electricity expenditure costs for prosumers can
be reduced effectively; (5) Compared with the single trading
mode, the electricity purchasing quantity from the grid at peak
hours can be decreased effectively as well.
