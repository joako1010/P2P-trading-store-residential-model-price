ABSTRACT
This paper presents a new prosumer-centric peer-to-peer (P2P) energy trading framework incorporating the
network-secure export–import limits, known as dynamic operating envelope (DOE), for prosumers considering their preferences to trade in the local market. In the proposed framework, a DOE-integrated energy
management algorithm is developed for prosumers considering electric vehicles (EVs) charge–discharge and
photovoltaic (PV) systems. The framework allows prosumers to operate within defined DOE, aligning with the
preferences of EV-owning prosumers by managing the desired EV battery charge levels at their departure. An
auction-based P2P energy trading mechanism for prosumers is proposed incorporating the allocated DOE to
ensure secure network operation and valuing prosumers’ preferences to benefit them. The proposed framework
is implemented on a 78-node low-voltage (LV) residential distribution network to evaluate its performance.
The results and comparative analyses verify the effectiveness of the proposed framework in addressing network
issues such as voltage and line congestion problems, meeting prosumers’ preferences in asset management, and
providing benefits to all participants in the energy trading process.

1. Introduction
1.1. Motivation and problem statement
With the proliferation of electric vehicles (EVs) and photovoltaic
(PV) systems as distributed energy resources (DERs) in the power
networks, prosumers, can actively participate in energy markets and
provide services to power grids. Recently, various studies have investigated new models for DER participation in energy markets. Emerging
market models such as peer-to-peer (P2P) energy trading, motivate
prosumers to trade energy with their peers to maximize benefits from
their DERs [1]. However, a high level of DER integration into lowvoltage (LV) residential distribution networks, with unrestricted import
and export, can challenge the integrity of the network in terms of node
voltage magnitude and line congestion problems, power imbalance
and system stability issues [2,3]. Moreover, with a high charging rate
and high penetration level, the charge/discharge of EVs connected to
networks might challenge the secure network operation [2]. Therefore,
appropriate network-aware trading mechanisms are required to benefit
prosumers financially from the market, while operational constraints of
the network and EV owners’ preferences are regarded.

In recent years, the P2P trading concept has been introduced in the
local energy market (LEM) for bilateral trade between peers through
the matching or negotiation process that can advantage prosumers to
achieve higher benefits from their DERs [4]. P2P trading in principle
facilitates the integration of DERs to a great extent into distribution
networks. However, P2P energy trading has various challenges in designing a suitable trading mechanism for prosumers. Uncontrolled P2P
energy trading in a distribution network can cause network operational
problems that can force the distribution system operator (DSO) to
restrict the trading by imposing a limit on the maximum import/export
in order to maintain network integrity. Moreover, the participation of
prosumers equipped with EVs can challenge P2P trading while managing their desires with EVs’ uncertain mobility behavior. In addition,
managing a large number of prosumers with various preferences in
P2P energy trading can impose control difficulty while coordinating
them for P2P trading [5]. Therefore, this paper aims to propose a P2P
energy trading framework for prosumers equipped with EVs and PV
systems to address the aforementioned challenges considering network
operational constraints, EVs’ uncertainties and prosumers’ preferences.

1.2. State-of-the-art review
Many P2P energy trading mechanisms have been developed in the
state-of-the-art to manage DERs in LV distribution networks. A P2P
energy trading for aggregated DERs is developed in [6] using the
alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) approach for prosumers’ enhanced profit with their flexibility. A blockchain-based P2P
energy trading framework is presented in [7] to clear the market with
payment settlement depending on the smart contracts. A cooperative
game-based P2P energy trading is developed to discourage incentivized
prosumers from not demanding any energy during peak hour [8]. A
secure P2P energy trading system is presented in [9] by using a multiobjective optimization for maximizing regional social welfare to obtain
the trading price and energy amount for EVs. A blockchain-based P2P
energy trading scheduling for EVs is studied in [10] by balancing
energy between the DSO and EVs in order to minimize the energy costs
of EVs and maximize the profit of the DSO. Nonetheless, these P2P
energy trading frameworks in [6–10] have not analyzed the impact of
energy trading on network operation.
Some studies in [11–18] propose network-constrained P2P trading
frameworks by either considering an additional charge for network
operation and usage or limiting the trade amount at the node experiencing any network issue. Considering the network usage charge for
maximizing profit of the DSO, a P2P energy trading is proposed for
prosumers in the active distribution system using ADMM method [11].
A network charge pricing mechanism has been developed in [12] to
benefit both prosumers and the DSO in P2P energy trading, respecting
the capacity constraints of the network. A voltage feasible market
clearing mechanism is presented in [13] for prosumers to trade in
the P2P energy market that assures safe network operation. A P2P
energy exchange mechanism is proposed in [5] that incorporates costs
for power loss, voltage regulation, and congestion with distribution
locational marginal price (DLMP) for each transaction. An iterative
auction-based matching approach is presented for the P2P energy
market in [4] that considers reputation and distance to model power
losses in energy transactions. Securing network constraints through
auction-based P2P energy trading for prosumers is developed in [14]
by managing their flexible demands. In [15], a decentralized P2P
energy market framework is proposed using ADMM that coordinates
prosumers’ trading with their independent nature respecting network
constraints. Maintaining market equilibrium and network constraints,
an optimal power flow (OPF)-based P2P energy market framework
is proposed in [16] using ADMM optimization. Yang et al. in [17]
have presented a decentralized P2P energy trading mechanism using
the ADMM method that maintains network constraints by maximizing
social welfare of the trade. A P2P energy trading based on coalition
graph game theory is studied in [18] that considers voltage constraints
to limit prosumers’ trade in the market. In the above studies, P2P
energy trading is managed considering network operational constraints
where either the DSO directly curtails or blocks energy trading of
prosumers to maintain the network integrity or assigns network usage
fee on top of the market clearing prices. However, these approaches
are not efficient in fully utilizing the grid capacity for DERs and might
impose extra costs on prosumers for network usage while trading in the
LEM. Besides, the DSO continuously interferes with the trading process
and accesses prosumers’ assets in order to resolve network constraints.
Recently, the dynamic operating envelope (DOE) has been introduced as a solution to limit prosumers’ power exports/imports, in
which the DSO plays a collateral role to ensure secure network operation for dynamic energy trading in the market [19]. In this context,
the DSO determines DOEs for prosumers depending on their intended
power exports/imports and network constraints so that prosumers can
manage their assets within the defined DOEs for secure trading without
breaching network constraints. Incorporating DOE, Petrou et al. [20]

1.3. Contributions and outline of the paper
There is a gap in designing a framework to secure prosumers’
trading in a prosumer-centric P2P energy market as well as manage the
energy of EVs’ charge–discharge and PV systems, which preserves the
network integrity while valuing prosumers’ preferences, benefits, and
fairness. Therefore, this paper proposes a new framework for coordinating prosumers equipped with EVs and PV systems using DOEs so that
prosumers can trade in the P2P energy market with their preferences
to benefit themselves and ensure the integrity of the network. In the
framework, a double auction mechanism is proposed for P2P energy
trading to enhance the performance efficiency of the market in which
market properties such as individual rationality, strong budget balance,
computational efficiency, and economic fairness and truthfulness would
be satisfied. The main contributions of the work are as follows:
• A new prosumer-centric P2P energy trading framework for prosumers is proposed that incorporates DOEs and considers the
preference of prosumers to trade in the market. The proposed
framework is developed based on a hybrid DER marketplace
model, consisting of the DSO to monitor the network operation
by allocating DOEs and a distribution market operator to manage
local energy trading of prosumers within allocated DOEs.
• In the proposed framework, the DOE is formulated to provide
an efficient allocation of hosting capacity whilst maximizing prosumers’ export/import power to/from the network in the presence
of EVs, as compared to many other existing DOE approaches,
without violating any network constraint. Considering EV charge–
discharge and PV systems, DOE-integrated energy management
is developed for prosumers, which allows them to increase the
utilization of PV system generations by prioritizing EV charge and
to fulfill the desired state of charge (SOC) of EV batteries at the
time of EVs’ departure.
• A DOE-integrated double auction-based market mechanism is
proposed to provide prosumer-centric P2P energy trading that
simultaneously benefits prosumers and ensures secure network
operation, as opposed to conventional double auction-based market mechanisms that prioritize social benefits. It is done by incorporating a prosumers’ preferred bidding process that satisfies
the auction properties in order to provide prosumers with equal
access to the local market with their preferences for P2P trading. The effectiveness of the proposed mechanism is evaluated
through a financial and comparative analysis by considering a
set of performance metrics to compare the proposed framework
with other methods such as trading with the grid, fixed import/export limit P2P trading without/with network issues and
network-constrained P2P trading.

2. Problem formulation
2.1. Framework overview
A prosumer-centric market framework is considered for P2P energy
trading in a smart residential LV distribution network with IoT facilities, where residential prosumers are equipped with DERs, such as EVs
and PV systems. The framework is a DER marketplace (in line with the
hybrid model framework in [25]) consisting of the DSO and a distribution market operator (DMO). In this model, the DSO monitors the
network operation to check whether any problems are caused during
the trading of prosumers for their intended power imports/exports and
manages the network within the operational constraints by allocating
DOEs for prosumers. On the other hand, the DMO administrates the
distribution market to manage local energy trading of prosumers considering their preferences within DOEs allocated by the DSO. A double
auction-based market mechanism is then proposed for P2P energy
trading for prosumers in the developed framework. Fig. 1 presents
the proposed framework of the DOE-based P2P energy market. As
shown in Fig. 1, the DMO responds to prosumers, by sending energy
price signals to prosumers who are willing to trade with their peers,
and prosumers submit their bids/offers including price and power
import/export quantities. The DMO communicates with the DSO by
forwarding prosumers’ bids/offers. The DSO continuously supervises
the network operation with the requested imports/exports and allocates
the DOEs for import/export limits for the prosumers to avoid anomalies
with the network operation so that prosumers can perform secure
trading with their peers. DOEs are communicated with the prosumers
in order to update their trading bids/offers and manage the energy
profiles of their assets. After receiving the updates of prosumers’ energy
bids/offers within the allocated DOEs, the DMO then starts the auction
mechanism for determining the matching peers as auction winners by
the proposed price and quantity matching algorithm as illustrated in
Section 3. The flowchart of the proposed network-secure P2P energy
market framework is depicted in Fig. 2. In the end, the results of
cleared prices and quantities are sent to the corresponding matched
prosumers. The winning prosumers are allowed to trade with their
peers in the P2P energy market, and losers in the auction can trade
with the upstream grid. The proposed framework enables prosumers to
manage their assets within their assigned DOEs using their management
systems without the direct control of the DSO. This indirect control
secures prosumers’ privacy by limiting DSO’s access to detailed energy
profile of their DER, as the DSO calculates DOEs using net generation/demand for each prosumer. Besides, the proposed framework
is prosumer-centric and facilitates all prosumers (buyers and sellers)
equally to participate in the market with their willingness and preferred
bids and offers for both energy and prices; provides fairness in terms of
having equal motivation in terms of getting trading access and payoff;
prioritizes prosumers to charge their EVs from the surplus of PV system
generation; fulfills EV owners’ preferences in terms of achieving the
desired SOC at their departure; and prosumers can manage the energy
of their assets individually in a decentralized manner and can restrict
data access for other entities [26].

3.2.2. Budget balance
The proposed trading framework is strongly budget-balanced, as all
matched players are equally paid off from their P2P energy transactions
by the proposed average pricing mechanism. No other third party gets
any financial benefit from the trade.
3.2.3. Computational efficiency
In the trading mechanism, the matching peers of prosumers are
chosen from the descended buyer and ascended seller winner matrices
as one-to-one trading between the highest price bid buyer and the
lowest price offer seller, and between the second highest price bid
buyer and the second lowest price offer seller, and so on until no
buyer or seller exists in their matrices. Therefore, the trading process
is formulated without requiring rigorous computational analysis, which
makes the proposed trading framework computationally efficient for
managing a large number of prosumers.

4.5. Computational efficiency
The performance of the proposed framework has been analyzed
using MATLAB software on a computer with Intel (R) Core (TM) i78700 CPU (3.20 GHz) and 32.0 GB of RAM. Table 3 presents the
computational efficiency of the proposed framework. As results in
Table 3 show, with the implementation of DOE computation, energy
management with DOE, and DOE-based P2P energy trading taking
1.326 s, 0.024 s, and 0.016 s, respectively, the average computation
time of the proposed framework for a Danish 78-node LV home distribution network is 1.366 s. Table 3 shows that the proposed framework
is computationally efficient and can be applied to near-real-time P2P
energy trading even for a large system.
5. Conclusion
This paper has proposed a new market framework for P2P energy
trading and management using DOEs as the network-secure export–
import limits by incorporating prosumers’ preferences to trade in the
market that benefits prosumers and ensures secure network operation. In the proposed framework, depending on the intended power
exports/imports and network constraints, the DOEs have been allocated for prosumers so that they can trade with their peers without
causing any voltage or congestion problems in the network. The prosumers’ energy management has been accomplished to manage EV
charge/discharge schedules by prioritizing EV charges from the surplus of PV system generation, updating the assets of prosumers, and
achieving the desires of EV battery charge levels at their departure.
The simulation results have shown that the proposed framework is able
