1. Introduction

1.1. Motivation and background

Demand for distributed generation (DG) systems is increas-
ng due to the advancements in power electronics, information
nd communication technologies, cost reductions in renewable
nergy systems (RESs) and energy storage systems, and poli-
ies regarding sustainability and environmental concerns. The
G structure is currently expected to increase decentralized and
ontrollable energy usage [1]. According to the European Union
nergy Technology Plan, small-scale behind-the-meter electricity
eneration will be at the heart of the future energy system [2].
ccording to the International Energy Agency’s report, the cost
f Photovoltaic (PV) systems has decreased by 80% in the last
en years. Furthermore, until 2030, an annual growth rate of

energy trade establish an energy management network through

as well as the energy trading with the grid (Peer-to-Grid, P2G) [5].

investment and operating cost, minimum reserve requirements,

optimal hybrid system. The goal of [22] was sizing the optimal
hybrid system using HOMER software. Moazzami et al. [23] rec-

particle swarm optimization method. The hybrid power system

particle swarm in [25]. Salman et al. [26] presented the non-linear
scheduling method to optimize BESS sizing for a wind-penetrated,

of PV panels and batteries were selected in [27] to reduce the

impact of optimal sizing was addressed in the literature through
examples of optimal size based on MILP in DG systems, in [29]

both load and, in [32] considering harmonic analyses in smart
household systems. However, the existing studies on optimal
PVs and BESS sizing in the P2P energy market were limited. The
optimal sizing of RESs in two microgrids and the annual cost were
maximized in [33] using a game theory approach. In [34], optimal
battery sizing was determined in various ownership structures in
the P2P sharing community. The study in [35] depicted storage
energy sharing model with an auction-based approach between

different residential units and shared facility controllers (SFCs).

1.3. Content and contributions

The purpose of this research is to provide the most appropriate
gain to users by optimizing the sizing of PV and BESS while
taking into account the cost function of capital, investment, and
maintenance in a market structure where energy can be sold as
P2P and P2G. As a result, it provides economic benefits to users
as well as a reduction in the return on investment period during
the operating period. The choice of PV/BESS and its economic
impact are examined between the situation where users trade
P2P energy and can only sell their energy as P2G. The contribution
of this study is threefold;

1. Taking capital, investment, and maintenance expenses into
account, a novel method is designed that enables users to
obtain the proper investment and make the most efficient
use of resources by utilizing optimal PV/BESS size in both
P2P and P2G cases.

2. Increased social welfare, complicated economic analysis in
a variety of case studies, and return on investment are
all examined in detail in relation to the proposed energy
management system software in a structure with domestic
participants where peer-to-peer energy trading exists.

3. By comparing the P2G and P2P cases, which are factored
into the initial investment expenses, the economic benefits
of P2P energy exchange, and the return on investment
advantage for prosumers and consumers are analyzed in
depth.

The mathematical model developed in the study is detailed in
Section 2, and the test case in the proposed methodology and the
simulation results are provided in Section 3. Section 4 includes
concluding remarks and future work.

. Conclusions

In this study, the optimal PV/BESS sizing in the P2P trading
as performed by maximizing the return of PV and BESS to the
sers and the net profit after deducting their costs. Users benefit
rom optimal PV and BESS sizing in terms of both social welfare
nd economic profit. Capital, replacement and maintenance costs
ere taken into account for precise sizing. Furthermore, the eco-
omic contribution of P2G and P2P energy exchange situations
ere examined. The use of P2P energy trading has resulted in
ubstantial benefits for both prosumers and customers in the
roposed scheme. In the case of P2P, a profit increase of 2% in
rosumer 1 and 20% in prosumer 2 were observed compared to
2G. Considering the total profit, it gained 45% more profit from
2P compared to P2G. As a future extension of this study or future
deas for new studies in this field, battery degradation of the BESS,
ncertainties related to renewable-based generations, and load
onsumptions together with market prices could be considered
or optimal sizing investments.
