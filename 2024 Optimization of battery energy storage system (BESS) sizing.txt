electricity bill savings. The breakdown of net present value exposes that under the same ownership, the optimal
BESS capacity is determined by different utilization mechanisms. Regarding the total net present value, it is

incentivizing user-owned BESS, promoting energy storage sharing, supporting shared BESS infrastructure, and
encouraging a diversity of electricity trading mechanisms to enhance the integration and efficiency of renewable
energy systems.

Under scenario 3, the PV-E will be first self-consumed, and then
sold to other peers through P2P trading if they are under electric
load shortage. After feeding other peers, the surplus PV-E will be
stored in the BESS before being sold to the utility grid. The pur-
pose of developing scenario 3 is to investigate the flexibility of
introducing P2P trading for IDUO-BESS.

(iv) Scenario 4 (P2P-SDUO-BESS) is developed as a scenario
compared to scenario 3 by facilitating the shared utilization for
UO-BESS. Specifically, the shared utilization for UO-BESS allows
the BESS-stored electricity to be traded through P2P trading
among all the peers, especially during peak period. In addition to
the trading process under scenario 3, the BESS-stored electricity
will be first discharged for self-consumption during peak period,
and then sold to other peers through P2P trading if they are under
electric load shortage. The purpose of developing scenario 4 is to
highlight the flexibility of SDUO-BESS compared to scenario 3.

(v) Scenario 5 (ESS-SDDO-BESS) is proposed as an alternative sce-
nario compared to other scenarios. In scenario 5, instead of the
UO-BESS, the DO-BESS is introduced to be installed in the
microgrid to demonstrate an innovative electricity market type.
Under scenario 5, the PV-E will be first self-consumed, and then
sold to other peers through P2P trading. After feeding other
peers, the surplus PV-E will be stored in the DO-BESS before being
sold to the utility grid. During peak period, the electricity trading
priority is for peers buying electricity from the DO-BESS first, and
the electric load shortage is then satisfied by the utility grid. The
purpose of developing scenario 5 is to propose an innovative
electricity trading mechanism as an alternative to other sce-
narios, which is believed to carry out insightful findings that can
enhance the development of ESS in the future.
